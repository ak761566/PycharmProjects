Completeness Analysis Explanation

Portico has developed a predictive process to make assumptions about what issues might exist and are not yet preserved in Portico. The predictive process primarily makes deductions based upon content we have preserved, however this is supplemented with checking the publisher's websites where we can. We use this information when tracking down missing content and we also present it here on these completeness pages.

Definitions of the Terminology

Complete Status: Our predictive analysis has a high degree of confidence that Portico has all the articles for this issue.

Incomplete Status: Our predictive analysis has a high degree of confidence that Portico does not have all the articles for this issue.

Unknown Status: Our predictive analysis is not confident enough to assess whether Portico has all the articles in this issue.

Articles: Portico has duplicate articles in the archive. We are slowly merging the duplicate articles in the archive. The number of "Articles" listed on these completeness pages is a de-duplicated number (thus it might not match the straight count of articles on the regular issue pages).

AUs: Archival Units that Portico has preserved -- occasionally different versions of an article are placed into separate Aus, rather than placed together into a single AU. This will be resolved over time as Portico merges them together. In the meantime, there may be a difference between the number of Aus (each AU is listed on the regular issue page) and the number of Articles.

At pub: If we can programmatically read the publisher’s website and count articles, we will list that number counted here.

Grade: As we preserve Archival Units, we grade them. For example, when an Archival Unit has a grade A, we believe we have all of the files and content as originally published. Whereas if an Archival Unit has a grade B, we believe we have sufficient content to present the entirety of the intellectual content, but we may be missing files (for example, we have a PDF version of the articles and a full-text XML version, but we believe we are missing referenced images – the PDF is a complete representation of the entirety of the intellectual content, but we are missing files and could not present a complete HTML rendition of the article.)

null: Sometimes the word "null" will appear in our volumes or issues, this occurs when we do not have a volume or issue number. It has no deeper meaning.

Years Preserved: This includes the earliest and latest years and it does not address gaps that may exist in between the two years.

Publication Pattern: This is part of our predictive analysis and is our best guess as to how many issues should be in each volume.

Have more questions or need data in a machine readable format? Contact us at info@portico.org and we'll be happy to talk with you further.
